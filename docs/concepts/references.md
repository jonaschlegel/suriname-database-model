> A working bibliography. These are real sources I've found useful or plan to read. Organised roughly by theme. Notes on what I've actually read vs. what's on the list.

## Surinamese History and Slavery

### Essential Reading (I've read these)

**Stipriaan, Alex van.** _Surinaams Contrast: Roofbouw en Overleven in een Caraïbische Plantagekolonie, 1750-1863_. Leiden: KITLV Press, 1993.
The standard economic history of Surinamese plantation slavery. Dense, quantitative, indispensable. Stipriaan used the same plantation registers I'm working with. His methodology for dealing with incomplete records is directly relevant.

**Oostindie, Gert.** _Roosenburg en Mon Bijou: Twee Surinaamse Plantages, 1720-1870_. Leiden: KITLV Press, 1989.
Micro-history of two specific plantations. Shows what's possible when you trace a single location through multiple source types. This is the level of detail I aspire to.

**Oostindie, Gert, and Emy Maduro.** "The Slavery Heritage of Suriname and the Netherlands." In _Facing Up to the Past: Perspectives on the Commemoration of Slavery from Africa, the Americas and Europe_, edited by Gert Oostindie, 57-79. Kingston: Ian Randle Publishers, 2001.
On memory and commemoration. Helps frame why this project matters beyond academic research.

**Hoefte, Rosemarijn.** _In Place of Slavery: A Social History of British Indian and Javanese Laborers in Suriname_. Gainesville: University Press of Florida, 1998.
Post-emancipation period. Not directly relevant to our 1700-1863 focus, but important for understanding what comes next and how labour systems transformed.

**Price, Richard.** _First-Time: The Historical Vision of an African American People_. 2nd ed. Chicago: University of Chicago Press, 2002.
Oral history of the Saramaka Maroons. Essential for understanding what the colonial archives _don't_ capture. A corrective to the plantation-centric view.

**Price, Richard, and Sally Price.** _Stedman's Surinam: Life in an Eighteenth-Century Slave Society_. Baltimore: Johns Hopkins University Press, 1992.
Abridged and annotated edition of John Gabriel Stedman's narrative. Primary source with excellent scholarly apparatus.

### On Dutch Colonial Slavery More Broadly

**Fatah-Black, Karwan.** _White Lies and Black Markets: Evading Metropolitan Authority in Colonial Suriname, 1650-1800_. Leiden: Brill, 2015.
On smuggling and informal economies. Reminds me that the official records I'm using captured only part of economic life.

**Emmer, Piet C., and Jos J.L. Gommans.** _The Dutch Overseas Empire, 1600-1800_. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2020.
General overview. Good for context, though Suriname is only one part.

**Brandon, Pepijn, Sabine"; Go, and Ulbe Bosma, eds.** _De Slavernij in Oost en West: Het Nederlandse Slavernijverleden_. Amsterdam: Spectrum, 2020.
Recent Dutch-language survey. Brings together VOC and WIC slavery scholarship.

### What I Haven't Read Yet But Should

**Klooster, Wim.** _Illicit Riches: Dutch Trade in the Caribbean, 1648-1795_. Leiden: KITLV Press, 1998.
On smuggling and informal trade. Important for understanding economic context.

**Postma, Johannes.** _The Dutch in the Atlantic Slave Trade, 1600-1815_. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1990.
The standard work on Dutch slave trading. Essential for understanding how people arrived in Suriname.

**Emmer, Pieter C.** _The Dutch Slave Trade, 1500-1850_. Translated by Chris Emery. New York: Berghahn Books, 2006.
Overview of Dutch involvement. Covers both VOC and WIC.

**Nimako, Kwame, and Glenn Willemsen.** _The Dutch Atlantic: Slavery, Abolition and Emancipation_. London: Pluto Press, 2011.
Recent synthesis. Good on the period around abolition.

**Jordaan, Han.** "The Curaçao Slave Market: From Asiento Trade to Free Trade, 1700-1730." In _Riches from Atlantic Commerce: Dutch Transatlantic Trade and Shipping, 1585-1817_, edited by Johannes Postma and Victor Enthoven, 219-257. Leiden: Brill, 2003.
On Curaçao as entrepôt. Some enslaved people came to Suriname via Curaçao.

### Maroon History

**Price, Richard.** _Alabi's World_. Baltimore: Johns Hopkins University Press, 1990.
18th-century Saramaka history from oral traditions and archives. Model for integrating different source types.

**Thoden van Velzen, H.U.E., and Wim Hoogbergen.** _Een zwarte vrijstaat in Suriname: De Okaanse samenleving in de achttiende eeuw_. Leiden: KITLV Press, 2011.
On the Ndyuka (Okanisi) Maroons. Dutch language.

**Bilby, Kenneth.** _True-Born Maroons_. Gainesville: University Press of Florida, 2005.
Comparative study including Suriname Maroons.

### Demography and Population

**Heuman, Gad, and Trevor Burnard, eds.** _The Routledge History of Slavery_. London: Routledge, 2011.
General reference but has chapters on Caribbean demography.

**Higman, B.W.** _Slave Populations of the British Caribbean, 1807-1834_. Baltimore: Johns Hopkins University Press, 1984.
Not Suriname, but methodologically important for working with slave registration data.

## The Archive and Colonial Records

### On Working with Colonial Archives

**Stoler, Ann Laura.** _Along the Archival Grain: Epistemic Anxieties and Colonial Common Sense_. Princeton: Princeton University Press, 2009.
Essential for thinking about what colonial archives do, how they were produced, what they reveal about colonial governance. Not specifically about slavery but highly relevant.

**Bastian, Jeannette Allis.** _Owning Memory: How a Caribbean Community Lost Its Archives and Found Its History_. Westport, CT: Libraries Unlimited, 2003.
About the US Virgin Islands but resonates with Caribbean archival issues generally. On the politics of whose records survive and who controls them.

**Schwartz, Joan M., and Terry Cook.** "Archives, Records, and Power: The Making of Modern Memory." _Archival Science_ 2 (2002): 1-19.
Foundational piece on archives as sites of power, not neutral repositories.

### On Slavery and the Archive Specifically

**Hartman, Saidiya.** "Venus in Two Acts." _Small Axe_ 12, no. 2 (2008): 1-14.
The essay I keep coming back to. About what we can and can't recover. About the violence of the archive itself. "How does one revisit the scene of subjection without replicating the grammar of violence?"

**Fuentes, Marisa J.** _Dispossessed Lives: Enslaved Women, Violence, and the Archive_. Philadelphia: University of Pennsylvania Press, 2016.
Methodological model for working with fragmentary records of enslaved people. Uses Bridgetown, Barbados archives. The chapter "Enraged, Diabolic Woman" on reading against the grain is particularly useful.

**Trouillot, Michel-Rolph.** _Silencing the Past: Power and the Production of History_. Boston: Beacon Press, 1995.
On how historical silences are produced. The chapter on the Haitian Revolution is famous, but the theoretical framework applies broadly.

**Berry, Daina Ramey.** _The Price for Their Pound of Flesh: The Value of the Enslaved, from Womb to Grave, in the Building of a Nation_. Boston: Beacon Press, 2017.
On how enslaved people were valued economically. Relevant because our plantation registers and inventories are economic documents.

**Morgan, Jennifer L.** _Reckoning with Slavery: Gender, Kinship, and Capitalism in the Early Black Atlantic_. Durham: Duke University Press, 2021.
On reproduction, kinship, and slavery's political economy. Challenges how we think about "demographic data."

## Digital Humanities and Slavery Databases

### Critical Approaches

**Johnson, Jessica Marie.** "Markup Bodies: Black [Life] Studies and Slavery [Death] Studies at the Digital Crossroads." _Social Text_ 36, no. 4 (2018): 57-79.
Critical examination of how slavery databases mark up Black bodies. Essential reading before building any database of enslaved people.

**Gallon, Kim.** "Making a Case for the Black Digital Humanities." In _Debates in the Digital Humanities 2016_, edited by Matthew K. Gold and Lauren F. Klein. Minneapolis: University of Minnesota Press, 2016.
On why mainstream digital humanities methods may not serve Black history well.

**Risam, Roopika.** _New Digital Worlds: Postcolonial Digital Humanities in Theory, Praxis, and Pedagogy_. Evanston: Northwestern University Press, 2019.
Broader than just slavery, but essential on decolonising digital methods.

**Brown, Vincent.** "Mapping a Slave Revolt: Visualizing Spatial History through the Archives of Slavery." _Social Text_ 33, no. 4 (2015): 134-141.
Reflections on creating the "Slave Revolt in Jamaica, 1760-1761" mapping project. On visualisation choices and their implications.

### Database Methodology

**Eltis, David, and David Richardson.** "A New Assessment of the Transatlantic Slave Trade." In _Extending the Frontiers: Essays on the New Transatlantic Slave Trade Database_, edited by David Eltis and David Richardson, 1-60. New Haven: Yale University Press, 2008.
Methodological essay on Slave Voyages database. How they handle uncertainty, imputation, data quality.

**Hall, Gwendolyn Midlo.** _Databases for the Study of Afro-Louisiana History and Genealogy, 1699-1860_. Baton Rouge: Louisiana State University Press, 2000.
One of the earliest slavery databases. Documentation of methodology and limitations.

**Hawthorne, Walter, Daryle Williams, and Dean Rehberger, dirs.** _Enslaved: Peoples of the Historical Slave Trade_. Michigan State University: Matrix Center for Digital Humanities & Social Sciences, 2024. https://enslaved.org/
The Enslaved.org project. Their documentation at https://docs.enslaved.org/ is essential reading.

**Zeuske, Michael.** "The Names of Slavery and Beyond: The Atlantic, the Americas and Cuba." In _The Second Slavery: Mass Slaveries and Modernity in the Americas and in the Atlantic Basin_, edited by Javier Laviña and Michael Zeuske, 15-50. Berlin: LIT Verlag, 2014.
On naming practices and what names tell us (and don't).

### The _Journal of Slavery and Data Preservation_

This peer-reviewed journal (https://jsdp.enslaved.org/) publishes datasets with accompanying scholarly articles. Directly relevant examples:

**Bergemann, Karl.** "Creating the Cape Colony Runaway Advertisements Datasets, 1830-1842." _Journal of Slavery and Data Preservation_ 6, no. 1 (2025). https://doi.org/10.14321/jsdp.6.1.0001

**Thomasson, Fredrik, Ale Pålsson, and Victor Wilson.** "Swedish Caribbean Colonialism: A Digital Archive of Swedish St. Barthélemy, 1785-1878." _Journal of Slavery and Data Preservation_ 6, no. 3 (2025). https://doi.org/10.14321/jsdp.6.3.0113

**Evans, Richard.** "Integrating the SlaveVoyages Databases: The Database of Combined Slave Trade Voyages." _Journal of Slavery and Data Preservation_ 6, no. 3 (2025). https://doi.org/10.14321/jsdp.6.3.0129

## PICO and Linked Data for Historical Persons

### Core Documentation

**Persons in Context (PICO) Model.** Official documentation. https://www.personsincontext.org/model/
The data model used by Dutch archives for historical person data. Core distinction between PersonObservation (raw record) and PersonReconstruction (derived identity). Essential for interoperability with Dutch LOD ecosystem.

**Person Name Vocabulary (PNV).** https://w3id.org/pnv
Structured vocabulary for Dutch (and similar) personal names. Handles prefixes, patronymics, and the complexities of historical naming.

### Academic Papers

**Woltjer, P. E., Zandhuis, I., Coret, B., Lindeman, M., Balkenende, J. D., Zijdeman, R. L., and Mourits, R. J.** "Persons in Context. A Model to Represent Observations and Reconstructions of Historical Persons in Linked Data." _Historical Life Course Studies_ 14 (2024): 105-125. https://doi.org/10.51964/hlcs19312
The foundational paper explaining PICO's architecture. On how to balance raw observation data with reconstructed identities.

**Mourits, R. J., Pepping, K., van Oort, T., Konings, P., and van Duijvenvoorde, B.** "Modelling the enslaved as historical persons: Extending the Persons in Context (PiCo) model to fit enslaved individuals." DH Benelux 2025, Amsterdam. https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.15586904
Extensions to PICO specifically for enslaved people. Directly relevant to our project. The authors are at IISH and Huygens Institute.

### Datasets Using PICO

**Paramaribo Ward Registers 1828-1847.** DataLegend. https://druid.datalegend.net/RJM/Paramaribo-Ward-Registers-1828-1847
The Ward Registers published as Linked Open Data using extended PICO. 2 million+ triples. This is our data, already in LOD form. Schema shows how enslavement relationships are modelled.

### Related Projects

**HDSC (Historical Database Suriname and the Caribbean).** Radboud University. https://www.ru.nl/hdsc/
The umbrella project for Surinamese historical data. Partners include Nationaal Archief, Anton de Kom Universiteit, Stichting voor Surinaamse Genealogie. They produce the datasets we want to integrate.

**CBG (Centre for Family History).** https://cbg.nl/
Developed and maintains PICO. Key player in Dutch genealogical data infrastructure.

**WieWasWie.** https://wiewaswie.nl/
Dutch genealogical platform that uses PICO. Shows how the model scales to millions of records.

### Tools

**A2A to PICO converter.** https://github.com/ivozandhuis/a2a-to-pico
Conversion from older A2A XML format. May be useful for archives still using A2A.

**PICO SHACL validation.** https://github.com/CBG-Centrum-voor-familiegeschiedenis/PiCo
SHACL shapes for validating PICO data. Useful for quality checking exports.

## Linked Places Format and Historical Geography

### Core Standards

**Linked Places Format (LPF).** https://github.com/LinkedPasts/linked-places-format
The international standard for historical gazetteers. Valid GeoJSON-LD with temporal scoping on names, geometries, types, and relations. Implemented by World Historical Gazetteer, Pelagios, Recogito.

**Nederlands profiel voor Linked Places (NL-LP).** Geonovum. https://geonovum.github.io/geooptijd/
Dutch profile of LPF for heritage data. Working draft (November 2025). Partners: Geonovum, Rijksdienst voor het Cultureel Erfgoed, KNAW Humanities Cluster, Netwerk Digitaal Erfgoed. Aligns with NEN 3610 geo-standards.

**Linked Pasts Ontology (LPO).** https://github.com/LinkedPasts/linked-pasts-ontology
Formal RDF layer for LPF. Defines attestation types (NameAttestation, TypeAttestation, RelAttestation, LinkAttestation) and temporal constructs.

**GeoJSON-T.** https://github.com/kgeographer/geojson-t
GeoJSON extension for temporal data. LPF is built on this.

### Platforms

**World Historical Gazetteer.** https://whgazetteer.org/
Main platform for LPF data. Upload, reconciliation, temporal visualisation. Run by University of Pittsburgh with international partners including KNAW Humanities Cluster.

**Pelagios Network.** https://pelagios.org/
International network for historical geo-data. Origins in ancient world gazetteers, now broader.

**HisGIS Netherlands.** https://hisgis.nl/
Dutch historical GIS. Potential source for administrative boundary data.

### Dutch Heritage Infrastructure

**Netwerk Digitaal Erfgoed.** https://netwerkdigitaalerfgoed.nl/
Dutch digital heritage network. Coordinates standards including NL-LP.

**Erfgoedatlas.** https://www.cultureelerfgoed.nl/onderwerpen/bronnen-en-kaarten/overzicht/erfgoedatlas
Heritage atlas from Rijksdienst voor het Cultureel Erfgoed. Example of temporal-spatial heritage data.

**Termennetwerk.** https://termennetwerk.netwerkdigitaalerfgoed.nl/
Dutch terminology network for heritage vocabularies.

### Academic

**Southall, Humphrey, Ruth Mostern, and Merrick Lex Berman.** "On Historical Gazetteers." _International Journal of Humanities and Arts Computing_ 5, no. 2 (2011): 127-145.
On building historical place-name databases. Foundational for LPF thinking.

## Primary Sources and Editions

### Printed Primary Sources

**Stedman, John Gabriel.** _Narrative of a Five Years' Expedition against the Revolted Negroes of Surinam_. Edited by Richard Price and Sally Price. Baltimore: Johns Hopkins University Press, 1988.
Critical edition of the famous 1796 account. The Prices' annotations are invaluable.

**Herlein, J.D.** _Beschryvinge van de Volk-Plantinge Zuriname_. Leeuwarden, 1718.
Early description of Suriname. Available in digitised form via Google Books.

**Hartsinck, Jan Jacob.** _Beschryving van Guiana of de Wilde Kust in Zuid-America_. Amsterdam, 1770.
Comprehensive 18th-century description. Often cited by later historians.

**Teenstra, M.D.** _De negerslaven in de kolonie Suriname en de uitbreiding van het Christendom onder de heidensche bevolking_. Dordrecht, 1842.
Pro-slavery perspective but useful data on plantation conditions near emancipation.

### Archival Collections

**Nationaal Archief, The Hague.** Archief Sociëteit van Suriname (1.05.03).
The main archive for Surinamese colonial records. Includes plantation registers, administrative correspondence. Finding aids at https://www.nationaalarchief.nl/

**Stadsarchief Amsterdam.** Archief van de Directie van de Sociëteit van Suriname.
Amsterdam-based records. Some overlap with Nationaal Archief holdings. WIC records also relevant.

**Surinaams Museum, Paramaribo.**
Holds materials not transferred to the Netherlands. Access can be challenging. Some digitisation efforts ongoing.

**Zeeuws Archief, Middelburg.**
Records of the Middelburgsche Commercie Compagnie (MCC), major slave trading company. Relevant for arrivals data.

## Dissertations (Recent)

These are harder to find but often contain the most detailed work:

**Schalkwijk, Marten.** "The Colonial State in the Caribbean: Structural Analysis and Changing Elite Networks in Surinam, 1650-1920." PhD diss., Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam, 2011.
On colonial governance structures.

**Stipriaan Luïscius, Alexandra A. van.** "Surinaams contrast: Roofbouw en overleven in een Caraïbische plantage-economie, 1750-1863." PhD diss., Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam, 1991.
Later published as the monograph cited above. The dissertation has more detail.

**To find:** Search ProQuest Dissertations & Theses Global for "Suriname slavery" and related terms. University repositories (UvA, VU, Leiden) also have open access dissertations.

## What I'm Missing

Looking at this list, I notice gaps:

1. **Surinamese scholarship in Sranan or Sranantongo.** My Dutch and English bias is showing.

2. **Recent PhD dissertations.** ProQuest and institutional repositories probably have relevant theses I haven't found.

3. **Grey literature.** NGO reports, heritage impact assessments, museum exhibition catalogues.

4. **Oral history collections.** These exist but aren't well catalogued.

6. **Maroon history.** Richard and Sally Price's work is a start, but there's more.

7. **Post-emancipation period.** Hoefte covers contract labour but there's more on the transition period.

## Where to Search

### Databases and Catalogues

- **Google Scholar** - Start here for recent articles. Set up alerts.
- **JSTOR** - Good for journal articles, especially older ones.
- **WorldCat** - For finding obscure books and checking library holdings.
- **KITLV/Brill** - Publisher specialising in Southeast Asian and Caribbean studies. https://brill.com/
- **ProQuest Dissertations** - For PhD theses. Often the most detailed work.

### Dutch-Specific

- **DBNL** (Digitale Bibliotheek voor de Nederlandse Letteren) - https://www.dbnl.org/ - Historical Dutch texts, some primary sources.
- **Delpher** - https://www.delpher.nl/ - Digitised Dutch newspapers, periodicals, books. Search for "Suriname" in historical newspapers.
- **KB (Koninklijke Bibliotheek)** - https://www.kb.nl/ - National library catalogue.

### Slavery-Specific

- **Enslaved.org** - https://enslaved.org/ - The hub. Also links to other projects.
- **Journal of Slavery and Data Preservation** - https://jsdp.enslaved.org/ - Peer-reviewed datasets.
- **Slave Voyages** - https://slavevoyages.org/ - For trans-Atlantic context.
- **UCL Legacies of British Slavery** - https://www.ucl.ac.uk/lbs/ - Compensation records. Some Dutch connections.

### Archival Finding Aids

- **Nationaal Archief** - https://www.nationaalarchief.nl/ - Main Dutch national archive.
- **Stadsarchief Amsterdam** - https://archief.amsterdam/ - WIC records.
- **TANAP** (Towards a New Age of Partnership) - VOC archives, but methodology relevant.

### Academic Networks

- **KITLV** (Royal Netherlands Institute of Southeast Asian and Caribbean Studies) - Now part of KNAW. Has working papers and conferences.
- **NiNsee** (National Institute Netherlands Slavery Heritage) - https://nfrse.nl/ - Dutch slavery research network.
- **Caribbean Studies Association** - Annual conferences, proceedings.
